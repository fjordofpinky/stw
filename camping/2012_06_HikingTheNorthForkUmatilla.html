2012_06_HikingTheNorthForkUmatillaWildness

Written by Steven Saturday, 23 June 2012 19:00

A couple weekends ago, Tara and I went on a nice little hike up the North Fork Umatilla River.  The North Fork Umatilla River is in a Wilderness area so only hiking and horseback riding are allowed.  We left home Saturday morning and drove up to the trailhead, which took about 1.5 hours.  We started out on the trail around mid-day and didn't really have a plan on how far to go.  This particular trail goes about 4 miles in and then starts climbing up a ridge, Coyote Ridge, and will eventually tie into the Tollgate Highway (Hwy 204).  Note: Ask Tara about the alien space craft that crashed near the Coyote Ridge Trail!  We got about 4 miles in where the trail starts to climb to go up to the ridge.  We decided to camp in the canyon as there would be little to no water once we left the river.  We camped a stones throw away from the river.  It was a very beautiful place - we will be going back.  It is amazing how different it feels than the South Fork Walla Walla River even though they are almost right next to each other.
