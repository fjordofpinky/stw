2011_07_LostineValley

Written by Steven Thursday, 04 August 2011 22:49

Last weekend, Tara and I went on our first backpacking trip of the summer.  We headed towards the Wallowa Mtns on Friday afternoon and arrived at the Two Pan trailhead around 3:30 or so.  After getting all of our stuff together and what-not, we got on the trail about 4:00 and started up the East Fork of the Lostine River.  The first couple of miles on the trail are the hardest.  Check out the picture below that shows the trail and the elevation gain (click on it to make it bigger).


We ended up hiking about 3.75 miles in and it was getting late enough that we wanted to get camp setup and dinner going before it got dark.  We camped down on the floor of the valley only a stones throw away from the river and we had a great view of Eagle Cap from the entrance to our tent.  We got up the next morning and decided to leave camp setup and just day hike up to Mirror Lake at the base of Eagle Cap.  We got up to the lake around lunch time and relaxed there for a few hours.  We wanted to climb Eagle Cap, but unfortunately we only made it about half way up (partly because we lost the trail in the snow and partly because I was getting blisters on my heels and did not want to totally destroy them!).  But we hung out on a nice overlook of the Lakes Basin.  We also had good views down Hurricane and Lostine Valleys.  We headed back towards camp and made some garlic pasta with lentils for dinner and called it an evening.  We had to limit our exposure when at camp because the mosquitoes were pretty bad.  We got up the next morning and headed back to the trailhead.  On our way home we stopped at the Little Bear Cafe in Wallowa, OR for lunch...and probably got enough grease, oil, and fat to make up for the calories we burned all weekend!
