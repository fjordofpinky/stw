2012_04_Waitsburg

Written by Steven Sunday, 15 April 2012 22:00

This weekend, Tara and I went for a reconnoitering trip.  I got some bags, saddlebags and tank bag, for Christmas.  I haven't had a chance to really try them out yet and we are hoping to do some motorcycle trips this summer.  Anyways, back to this weekend, we decided to try the new equipment on a short & easy trip...hence Waitsburg (only about a 40-45 minute ride from home).  We got everything loaded up and headed off.  It was lunch time to we decided to stop at Tara's favorite restaurant for some food (taco, tamale and enfrijoladas).  We then made our way through the wheat fields north of Walla Walla on Lower Waitsburg Road.  We arrived in Waitsburg and parked by a little cafe called the Coppei Cafe (there is a small creek that starts in the Blue Moutains and runs through Waitsburg called Coppei Creek).  Tara got a cup of coffee and a brownie muffin (with a Reese's peanut butter cup in the middle - the baker is trying some new things!) and I just sipped some refreshing water.  We then visited the local hardware/fishing supply/pet adoption store housed in the oldest brick building in Waitsburg (1882 I believe).  We then decided we should check in to our motel room.  We got everything organized and decided to hit the big metropolis of Dayton, WA (about 9 miles NE).  We walked through downtown and checked out the Weinhard Hotel which has a roof garden.  We also visited the local bookstore and bought a used book about free speech during wartime (US History).  We decided to head back to Waitsburg to have some dinner at the Coppei Cafe.  BUT, just as we were putting our helmets on a seasoned biker guy pulled up behind us on a Victory motorcycle and we talked for about 30 minutes or so.  He talked to us about his half dozen or so motorcycles and a run in with an angry deer that resulted with him having to have brain surgery.  He also informed us that he has the same kind of bike as me, but in the 600cc version...and he has had it up to about 140 mph.  After our nice chat we arrived at the Coppei Cafe and had some delicious bagel pizza and played games while people learned the Touchet Two-Step.  We had a wonderful night's sleep and then rode back to Walla Walla this morning.  We got back in time to have some doughnuts at Popular Doughnuts before Tara went to work with some volunteers and plant some native plants.  Our trip was a success, reconnoiter complete, the bags worked great and we will be planning some more motorcycle adventures soon!
