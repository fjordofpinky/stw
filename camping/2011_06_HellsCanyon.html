2011_06_Hell's Canyon

Written by Steven Wednesday, 08 June 2011 20:05

Last weekend, Tara and I went down to Hell's Canyon to celebrate Eric's birthday.  We drove down after work on Friday and went up and over the hill from Whitebird, ID to Pittsburgh Landing in pitch dark.  If you have ever been over that road you know that is good and bad.  We had a good night's rest and got up the next morning and had a wonderful breakfast and then headed off on a "little" hike.  It ended up that the hike to the trailhead to start the hike was longer than what we actually hiked on the trail.  We saw a Western Rattlesnake along the way and quite a few cool wildflowers.  We got back to camp and relaxed the rest of the afternoon by sleeping, reading, and snacking.  Megan and Adric arrived Saturday night after attending Adric's grandmother's funeral early that day.  Sunday morning we got up and had a quick breakfast and headed out to get on the boat. We had a blast on the jet boat.  We didn't get to make it all the way up to Hell's Canyon Dam because the river was running at around 50,000 cfs (or about 2.24 million gallons per minute).  We still went up around 25 miles or so.  Check out the pictures and video below.  You can also download all of the pictures from the trip below the video.
