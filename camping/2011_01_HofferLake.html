2011_01_HofferLake

Written by Steven Monday, 28 February 2011 20:52

Almost a month ago, Tara and I went on a snowshoeing trip with one of my coworkers, Troy.  It was the first time that either Tara or I had been up to Anthony Lakes Ski Resort (If you ever get a chance you should go because it has GREAT views of the Elkhorn Mtns!).  We snowshoed into Hoffer Lake which is about 1.25 miles from the resort.  Unfortunately, we missed the well-marked trail and ended up cross-countrying pretty much the whole way to Hoffer Lake.  Instead of have a nice established trail we were side sloping and breaking trail, going in and around trees, having a peachy time of it!  After we got to the lake we dug out a HUGE area for our tent.  We had walls that were over 6 feet tall - we could hardly tell if the wind was blowing.  We had a great night and if I would have been a little more ambitious I would have gotten my camera out in the middle of the night and taken some star pictures.  We saw more stars than either of us could remember.  It was really amazing!  The next morning we got up and started up one of the peaks.  After summiting a small knoll in front of the actual peak, I decided to stop and take some photographs and try out my new GoPro HD Camera.  Tara and Troy continued onward and upward and eventually summited Lee's Peak.  I met them back at camp and we had an easy hike back on the well-established and relatively flat trail.  Once we reached the X-country ski area (which they groom) we got yelled at for snowshoeing on the X-country ski trail and our tracks were going to cause someone to have an accident (wimpy X-country skiers).   We got back to the car and had a nice drive back, unfortunately as we went through the La Grande valley the Elkhorns and the Wallowas were covered by clouds.
