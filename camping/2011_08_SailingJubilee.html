2011_08_Sailing Jubilee

Written by Steven Monday, 22 August 2011 16:27

Last weekend, Tara and I went camping with Billy, Matt, and Jen.  We ended up staying at a little campground called Woodland, just on the other side of the Blue Mtns summit.  We had a fun time camping and making camp suppers and talking late into the night (at least late by some people's standards...Tara).  The next morning we got up and had a wonderful breakfast of Turkey Bacon, Egg and Cheese biscuits.  Then Tara and I went off to collect a pick-up full of wood for the winter.  After a couple of hours of logging (I'm a lumberjack and I'm ok...) we met the others up at Jubilee Lake for a picnic lunch and some sailing on the lake.  We ate and then spent a few hours sailing around the lake.  Unfortunately, Jubilee Lake isn't the best spot to go sailing because it has very sporadic wind which makes it boring and then exciting and then boring again.  I have wanted to attach my camera to our sailboat for a while now and finally got the chance.  During one of the wind gusts my suction cup mount fell of the boat (thankfully the camera wasn't attached) so I only got one camera angle for the video.  Next time when the wind is better I want to try some more angles.  We had a fun time and came back to get a free dinner at the Muddy Frogwater Festival here in Milton-Freewater.
