2012_06_HikingTheNorthForkWallaWallaRiver_Trail3222

Written by Steven Friday, 22 June 2012 18:34

Last weekend, Tara and I went on a hike with Laura who is a member of the Native Plant Society.  Tara is leading a Native Plant Society hike next weekend and Laura wanted to show us the trail and look at some plants - neither of us had ever been on this trail before.  We hiked along a forest service road for about a mile and then reached trail #3222 which crosses the forest service road.  Trail #3222 is the North Fork Walla Walla River Trail.  Where we met up with the trail is a very short distance from the headwaters of the North Fork Walla Walla River.  Laura waited at the road while Tara and I went on a little trip down the trail.  It's pretty steep, but the river is gorgeous!  We are definitely going to go back and hopefully hike down the North Fork and then go up the ridge dividing the North and South Forks and drop down Bear Creek into the South Fork drainage and hike out to Harris Park.  Below are some pictures I took of the native plants.
