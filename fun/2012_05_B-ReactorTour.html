2012_05_B-ReactorTour

Written by Steven Sunday, 06 May 2012 19:00

Yesterday, Tara and I went on the B Reactor Tour at the Hanford Nuclear Reservation just outside of the Tri-Cities.  It was a pretty rocking tour.  We were 10 feet away from the front of the reactor's face, we saw the control room, saw Enrico Fermi's office, and learned a lot about how the reactor was built and operated.  Check out the photos and video below!

On the bus heading to Hanford!
 Approaching the B-Reactor.  You can see both B
and C reactors in this picture (B on the left and C is on the right but it has been "cocooned."
 Train that used to carry irradiated uranium fuel rods
 from the reactor to the T building where they isolated
the plutonium from the uramium. Behind the engines you can see the cask that carried the uranium from one location in Hanford to another.



Sign outside of the B Reactor with the dates it was operational.
 Map showing the Hanford Site.  The B Reactor is the farthest to the left.
 The front of B Reactor.  There are 2004 process tubes.  The process tubes are where they load the uranium fuel rods.  All of the pipes on the left and right are feeding cool water from the Columbia River to keep the reactor at the right temp.



The two silver containers on either side of the picture are B Reactors secondary hydraulics.  They are filled with river rock from the Columbia River and are lifted into the air and held with hydraulic pumps.  If the power went out and the hydraulic pumps when down these containers, which are lifted into the air, provide the pressure, as they fall to the ground, to operate the horizontal control rods for the reactor.
 In case you need to brush up on what nuclear fission is!

Water pipes that brought water from the Columbia River to cool the reactor.  The access ports are open because every few years the Russians come over and check that we are not running the B Reactor to produce plutonium.
